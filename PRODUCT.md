# Product

## Register

product

## Users

LaunchLens AI is for solo founders, indie builders, small SaaS teams, and technical product managers who need to turn a rough product idea into a concrete go-to-market workspace. The primary user is usually working alone or in a very small team, under time pressure, and needs a tool that helps them think clearly before they overbuild.

## Product Purpose

LaunchLens AI turns a founder brief into an editable workspace for positioning, target users, pain mapping, MVP scope, pricing hypotheses, launch work, validation assumptions, evidence, decisions, and exportable handoff material.

Success means the user can move from idea to decision-quality execution: generate a plan, edit it, test assumptions, record evidence, synthesize evidence-grounded AI briefs, save or share the work safely, and export a useful product handoff.

## Brand Personality

Precise, credible, and product-minded.

The interface should feel like a serious AI product engineering workspace, not a marketing demo, not a toy generator, and not a generic admin template. It should give reviewers confidence that the builder understands product workflow, AI safety boundaries, commercial readiness, and full-stack execution.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS pages with purple-blue glow, gradient hero copy, and decorative glass panels.
- Washed-out pastel dashboards where every metric is a tinted status card.
- Overly playful generator UIs that make strategic product work feel unserious.
- Dense enterprise software that hides the main workflow behind configuration noise.
- Decorative motion, fake metrics, or visuals that do not help the user's task.

## Design Principles

1. Workflow before decoration: the generate -> edit -> validate -> decide -> save/share/export loop must stay obvious.
2. Evidence earns trust: validation state, citation counts, provider mode, fallback state, and privacy boundaries should be legible without shouting.
3. Calm density: use compact, structured surfaces for repeated work, with enough whitespace to keep decisions readable.
4. Commercial credibility: billing, readiness, and account surfaces should feel like a real SaaS control plane, not a portfolio mock.
5. Secret-safe by default: UI copy and structure should reinforce that provider secrets, owner credentials, private evidence, and billing IDs are not exposed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast, focus visibility, keyboard navigation, touch target sizing, and reduced-motion behavior. The product should remain usable without API keys, without cloud storage, on mobile widths, and in local-only mode.
